# Morning Focus Note

Date: 2026-04-11
Type: journal

I noticed again that my best focused work happens early in the morning, especially before checking messages. When I start the day by opening WhatsApp, email, or random tabs, I feel mentally fragmented for at least an hour.

Today I worked on a hard problem first and got more done in 45 minutes than I usually do in two distracted hours later in the day. The work felt lighter once I stopped switching contexts.

There is a pattern here: deep work seems less about motivation and more about protecting the first block of attention before the day gets noisy. My energy is not always higher in the morning, but my mind is cleaner and less reactive.

I also noticed that when I decide on the task the night before, I start faster and resist avoidance more easily. When the task is vague, I drift toward stimulation instead of effort.

Open question: is the real driver time of day, lower input volume, or having a pre-decided target?
