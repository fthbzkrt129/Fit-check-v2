## Abstract

This journal note argues that the best focused work block tends to happen early in the day, especially before reactive inputs like messages or email fragment attention.

## Key Claims

- Early-day work produces higher-quality concentration than later reactive work.
- Message checking appears to fragment attention and reduce the quality of the first work block.
- Task clarity from the night before lowers avoidance and makes hard work easier to start.
- The pattern may be driven by cleaner attention rather than simply higher energy.

## Notable Entities

- Morning work block
- Reactive inputs such as WhatsApp, email, and random browsing

## Notable Concepts

- [[deep-work]]
- task clarity

## Open Questions

- Is the main lever time of day, lower input volume, or pre-decided task clarity?
- How much of the improvement comes from environment design versus intrinsic motivation?

## Tensions And Contradictions

- The note suggests morning is best for focused work, but it also hints that reduced incoming noise may matter more than the clock itself. This remains unresolved.
