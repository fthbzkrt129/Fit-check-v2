# Deep Work

Deep work refers to periods of sustained, undistracted concentration on cognitively demanding tasks.

## Current Synthesis

In this wiki, deep work currently appears less like a willpower problem and more like an attention-protection problem. The strongest early evidence suggests that protecting the first clean block of attention may matter more than trying to force focus after reactive inputs have already fragmented the day ([[2026-04-11-morning-focus-note]]).

## Supporting Evidence

- A journal note on 2026-04-11 reports significantly better performance before checking messages, email, or random tabs ([[2026-04-11-morning-focus-note]]).
- The same note suggests that deciding the target task the night before reduces avoidance and startup friction ([[2026-04-11-morning-focus-note]]).
